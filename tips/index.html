Goa Like a Pro

Planning

Best Time to Visit

November - February

Peak season. Pleasant weather (20-30°C), festivals, and vibrant nightlife. Book early for best rates!

March - May

Off-season. Hotter (30-40°C) but fewer crowds. Great for budget travelers. Monsoon starts late May.

June - October

Monsoon season. Lush greenery, dramatic waterfalls (Dudhsagar at full flow!), and lowest prices.

Essentials

What to Pack

Light Clothes Cotton & linen for the heat
Sunscreen SPF 50+ essential
Sunglasses Polarized recommended
Cash Small vendors prefer cash

Important

Safety Tips

Driving & Road Safety

Goa's roads can be busy. Always wear seatbelts, don't drink and drive (strict laws), and use registered taxis like us for safety. Avoid riding motorcycles at night without proper gear.

Beach Safety

Swim only at patrolled beaches. Watch for strong currents at Vagator and Arambol. Don't swim under the influence. Apply sunscreen regularly - the Goan sun is stronger than expected!

Water & Food

Drink bottled water only. Goan cuisine is delicious but can be spicy - start with small portions. Fresh seafood is excellent but ensure it's from reputable places.

Valuables & Documents

Keep photocopies of ID and passport. Don't flash cash or electronics on the beach. Use hotel safes for valuables. Keep emergency contact numbers saved.

Insider Tips

Go Local

🍽️ Eat local: Try Goan fish curry, vindaloo, and bebinca. Visit shacks away from the beach for authentic food at local prices.

🌅 Sunrise vs Sunset: North Goa is for sunset parties. South Goa is for peaceful sunrises.

🛍️ Shopping: Anjuna flea market (Wednesdays) is great for souvenirs. Flea at Mapusa (Fridays) for local goods.

⏰ Timing: Visit popular beaches early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds. Dudhsagar is best visited in the morning.

📱 SIM Card: Get a local SIM (Jio/Airtel) at the airport for cheap data and calls.
